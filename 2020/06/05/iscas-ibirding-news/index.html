Release of iBirding - Learn about our exciting achievement!

Posted on 2020-06-05 In Study Disqus:

Today, the iBirding database and mobile application were launched to provide a technology solution to shape wetlands conservation policy along China’s coast. During past one and a half year, I have been working on this inspired and challenging project. I am very glad to contribute to waterbird recognition and databases with techniques about computer vision, cloud computing server and latest Javascript application.

Furthermore, I am so appriciate with this opportunity to protect our natural environment. “There is a difference between a few pairs of eyes and a few million pairs of eyes. So this platform is effective because regardless of whether they are professionals or amateurs, they are knowingly and unknowingly helping in researchers’ data collection work,” said Zhang Yue, president of Aita Foundation for Animal Protection, who hosted the online event, and professor of Beijing Forestry University.

Importantly, the database has been developed in consultation with the eBird database at Cornell University in the US, allowing data to be shared, thus strengthening both applications and recognizing that many of these shorebirds - such as the Bar-tailed Godwit, which breeds in Alaska and uses China’s coast as a vital stopover site - are part of the natural heritage shared by both China and the United States.

“Data that we can share is going to strengthen the integrity of both applications—iBirding and eBird—and it’s going to facilitate better informed decision making in both of our countries.” —Wendy Paulson, Chairman, Bobolink Foundation

Welcome try our application and give a hand to help us protect waterbirds not only in China but also all around the world.
